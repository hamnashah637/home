Kelly Wearstler

Kelly Wearstler is an acclaimed American designer known for her bold, eclectic style, which blends modern and classical influences in interior design, furniture, and décor. She has garnered a reputation as a trendsetter in the luxury design world, working on high-end residential and commercial projects, as well as boutique hotels.

Early Life and Education:

.Born on November 21, 1967, in Myrtle Beach, South Carolina.

.Graduated from the Massachusetts College of Art with a degree in interior and graphic design.

Career Highlights

. Hospitality Design: She became widely known for her work on boutique hotels, particularly for the Viceroy Hotels and Residences. Her work in hospitality helped redefine modern luxury interiors.

. Residential Projects: Wearstler has designed high-end residential spaces for notable clients, including celebrities and entrepreneurs, blending different periods, textures, and colors.

. Product Lines: She has her own collections of furniture, home accessories, lighting, wall coverings, and fabrics. Wearstler also released jewelry and fashion lines that reflect her interior design aesthetics.

Design Philosophy:

.Known for mixing textures, patterns, and vibrant colors with a touch of vintage and modern elements

.Wearstler describes her style as "theatrical" and "unexpected."

Awards and Recognition:

. Named one of Architectural Digest's "Top 100 Designers."

.Included in Time magazine’s list of the "100 Most Creative People in Business."

.Frequently featured in design publications like Elle Decor, Vogue, and The New York Times.
